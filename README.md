# BC_DLD
Many historical simulations and future climate projections are available from Earth System Models (ESMs), but their coarse spatial resolution limits their usefulness and usability for assessing local-scale climate changes and impacts. Here, we use a novel deep learning downscaling framework named BC_DLD to downscale daily precipitation, daily maximum, and minimum temperature for the U.S. Northeast from four Coupled Model Intercomparison Projects (CMIP6) ESMs (GFDL-ESM4, HadGEM3, EC-Earth3, and MRI-ESM2) that differ in their global equilibrium climate sensitivities (ECS). This framework employs ERA5 reanalysis data at ~25 km resolution and gridMET data at ~4 km for model training and bias correction. The downscaled datasets, stored in NetCDF format, are publicly available on Zenodo (DOI:10.5281/zenodo.19076857), for the historical (1985–2014) and future (2040–2099) periods at ~4 km (1/24-degree resolution) for the SSP5-8.5 scenario. This high-resolution BC_DLD dataset enables locally relevant, fine-resolution analyses of extreme precipitation and heat events in the U.S. Northeast, supporting research and applications related to climate impact and adaptation in the water, energy, and agriculture sectors.The regridding of CMIP6 model outputs to the reference dataset is performed using CDO, and the empirical quantile delta mapping bias correction method (E-QDM) is developed in Python (version 3.10). The CDO workflow and Python script used in E-QDM development are publicly available under this repository.
